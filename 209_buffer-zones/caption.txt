Principle Nº 209: Buffer Zones

Plan slack to handle uncertainty. A system sized exactly to average demand looks efficient right up until demand isn't average, which is most of the time, since averages hide the spikes.

Ask yourself: Where are you running too tight to absorb a normal shock?
