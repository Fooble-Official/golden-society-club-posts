Principle Nº 213: Decision Filters

Pre-commit to simplifying choices. A filter only saves effort if it's specific enough to actually eliminate options before you evaluate them, a vague value like 'be selective' still leaves you weighing every choice individually.

Ask yourself: What standing rule would save you 20 decisions this month?
