Principle Nº 221: Bounded Rationality

Limit scope to make decisions tractable. Trying to account for every variable feels responsible, but it usually just produces paralysis, because complexity grows faster than your ability to reason about it.

Ask yourself: What are you trying to solve at too broad a scope?
