Principle Nº 296: Framing Effect

The same fact produces different decisions depending on how it is framed. In a classic study, doctors recommended a treatment more often when told it had "90% survival" than "10% mortality", same fact, different decisions.

Ask yourself: How would the decision change if I framed it the opposite way, and which frame is the truer one?
