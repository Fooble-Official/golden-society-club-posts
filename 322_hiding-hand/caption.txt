Principle Nº 322: Hiding Hand

Underestimating difficulty can paradoxically unleash creativity that overcomes it. Hirschman studied World Bank-funded projects and found that those that succeeded often did so because original cost estimates were wildly low.

Ask yourself: What worthwhile project would I never start if I fully understood its difficulty, and is that exactly why I should start it?
