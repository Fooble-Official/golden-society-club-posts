# NEW principles to add to the journal / app (329-365)

37 new deep, tested principles added to reach 365. Each has a computable equation (verified against a worked example). Titles are plain descriptive names (not named after people). Add these to the journal's principle + equation source.

## 329. The Queue Equation  ·  Operations & Flow
- Principle: Items stuck in any system equal how fast they arrive times how long they stay.
- Equation: WIP = Throughput × Lead Time

## 330. The Experience Curve  ·  Economics & Scale
- Principle: Every time you double your total output, your cost per unit drops by a fixed percent.
- Equation: New Cost = Old Cost × Learning Rate ^ (Doublings)

## 331. The Mirror Principle  ·  Organization & Execution
- Principle: Your product's structure ends up mirroring the shape of your org chart.
- Equation: Coordination Interfaces = Teams × (Teams − 1) ÷ 2

## 332. The Late-Project Trap  ·  Organization & Execution
- Principle: Adding people to a project that's already late will make it later, not sooner.
- Equation: Comms Overhead = People × (People − 1) ÷ 2

## 333. The Square-Root Rule  ·  Organization & Execution
- Principle: The square root of your headcount produces about half of all the output.
- Equation: Top Producers = √(Total People)

## 334. Start Simple, Grow Complex  ·  Systems & Complexity
- Principle: Every working complex system grew out of a working simple one.
- Equation: Survival Odds = Simplicity at Launch ÷ Complexity at Launch

## 335. Bullwhip Effect  ·  Operations & Flow
- Principle: Small swings in customer demand amplify into huge swings the further upstream you go.
- Equation: Upstream Swing = Demand Swing × Tiers Amplified

## 336. Cost Disease  ·  Economics & Scale
- Principle: Work that resists automation gets more expensive as the rest of the economy speeds up.
- Equation: Cost Growth = Economy Wage Growth − Sector Productivity Growth

## 337. The Build-vs-Buy Boundary  ·  Economics & Scale
- Principle: Build it in-house only until buying it on the market costs you less.
- Equation: Firm Boundary: Internal Cost = Market Transaction Cost

## 338. The Rebound Effect  ·  Economics & Scale
- Principle: Make a resource cheaper to use and you'll often use far more of it, not less.
- Equation: Total Use = (Tasks × Use per Task) ÷ Efficiency

## 339. The Problem-Preservation Trap  ·  Behavioral & Institutional
- Principle: Institutions quietly preserve the very problem they were built to solve.
- Equation: Preservation Risk = Revenue from the Problem ÷ Revenue from Solving It

## 340. The Fence Principle  ·  Behavioral & Institutional
- Principle: Don't tear down a rule until you understand why someone built it.
- Equation: Removal Safety = Understanding of Its Original Purpose (rate 1-10)

## 341. Bad Drives Out Good  ·  Behavioral & Institutional
- Principle: When buyers can't see quality and the price won't move, bad drives out good.
- Equation: Adverse Selection = Quality Opacity × Price Rigidity

## 342. Social Loafing  ·  Organization & Execution
- Principle: The bigger the team, the less effort each person actually contributes.
- Equation: Effort per Person = Group Output ÷ (People × Solo Output)

## 343. Winner's Curse  ·  Risk & Uncertainty
- Principle: In a real auction, beating everyone usually means you were the one who misjudged the value.
- Equation: Overpayment = Winning Bid − Average Bid

## 344. The Focal Point  ·  Behavioral & Institutional
- Principle: When people can't talk it out, they still converge on the choice that feels obvious to everyone.
- Equation: Coordination Odds = Focal Salience (rate 1-10)

## 345. Minimum Efficient Scale  ·  Operations & Flow
- Principle: There's a smallest size you have to reach before your cost per unit stops falling.
- Equation: Unit Cost = (Fixed Cost ÷ Volume) + Variable Cost

## 346. Aggregation Theory  ·  Strategy & Market Power
- Principle: The internet flipped power to whoever owns the customer, not whoever makes the product.
- Equation: Aggregation Power = Demand Owned ÷ Supply Owned

## 347. Cornered Resource  ·  Strategy & Market Power
- Principle: Sometimes the whole moat is one asset your rivals simply cannot get their hands on.
- Equation: Cornered Resource Value = Asset Desirability × Exclusivity

## 348. Process Power  ·  Strategy & Market Power
- Principle: Some advantages live in how you work, refined so long that copying them takes rivals years.
- Equation: Process Power = Improvement Rate × Years Compounded × Copy Difficulty

## 349. Switching-Cost Power  ·  Strategy & Market Power
- Principle: Customers do not stay because they love you; they stay because leaving costs more than staying.
- Equation: Lock-In = Switching Cost ÷ Annual Value

## 350. Regression to the Mean  ·  Risk & Uncertainty
- Principle: Extreme results are mostly luck wearing a costume, and luck does not repeat.
- Equation: Expected Next = Mean + Correlation × (Observed − Mean)

## 351. The Aggregation Paradox  ·  Risk & Uncertainty
- Principle: A trend that holds in every group can flip completely once you pool the groups together.
- Equation: Paradox Risk = Segment Mix Imbalance (rate 1-10)

## 352. Diffusion of Innovations  ·  Network & Growth
- Principle: New ideas spread first through a brave few, then accelerate as everyone copies the crowd.
- Equation: New Adopters = Innovation Pull + Imitation Pull × (Adopted ÷ Market)

## 353. Real Options  ·  Risk & Uncertainty
- Principle: Keeping your options open is not indecision; under uncertainty it is an asset you can price.
- Equation: Option Value = (Upside × Probability) − Cost to Hold Open

## 354. The 150 Limit  ·  Organization & Execution
- Principle: You can hold about 150 real relationships in your head; past that, you need structure instead of memory.

## 355. OODA Loop  ·  Asymmetry & Timing

## 361. Status Goods  ·  Behavioral & Institutional

## 362. Value-Chain Mapping  ·  Strategy & Market Power

## 363. Two-Sided Subsidy  ·  Strategy & Market Power

## 364. Optimal Bet Sizing  ·  Risk & Uncertainty

## 365. Veil of Ignorance Pricing  ·  Behavioral & Institutional
- Principle: Set policies you would still accept without knowing which side you will land on.
- Equation: Fairness Score = Acceptability to Both Sides ÷ 10
